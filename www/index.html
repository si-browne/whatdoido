After an accident you must



1. Stop your car - Failing to do so is an offence under the Road Traffic Act 1991.

2. Switch off the engine and turn on your hazard lights.

(if your car is parked do not start the engine)

3. Do not interfere with any damaged property.








First Aid



1. Secure the area around you, to prevent any further harm. Do not stand on the road. Be careful of oncoming traffic.

2. Check for any injuries to yourself, passengers or others & assist as appropriate.

3. Call an ambulance on 999 if necessary.

4. Remain CALM. Try to breathe and avoid angry confrontations.








Criminality & Blame



1. Establish the facts. Avoid accepting blame... until you know PRECISELY what happened. This could count against you later on.

2. Any accident should be reported to the police within 24 hours, obtain a police reference number.

3. If you suspect there has been a crime, contact the police for assistance:

-101 non-emergency.
-999 emergency.







Criminality & Blame



1. Share your name and address with everyone involved if the accident caused damage or injury.
-The law states you must do this.




2. Call 999 immediately if someone leaves the scene of the car accident without providing their:
-Car registration number
-Name and address
-Details of the registered keeper if different from the driver
